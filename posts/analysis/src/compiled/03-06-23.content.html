Let \(p_n\) denote the n-th prime. The prime number theorem states that \(p_{n}\approx n\log n\). Let \(p_n\#\) denote the product of the first n primes (the “primorial”). Then, \(p_n\# \approx e^{n}\) [source: wikipedia] \(numdivs(n\#) = 2^{n}\) because \(n\# = 2\cdot 3 \cdot 5 \cdot \cdots \cdot p_n\).

so I guess \(2_{n} \ll e^{n}\), but is still exponential.
